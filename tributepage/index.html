Hayao Miyazaki

One of the greatest japanese animator and director of all time

Hayao Miyazaki

1941-1947

  • Born January 5th,1941
  • grew up during warring period of the second world war

Early Life:1947-1962

    miyazaki's father was the owner of a family owner airplane manifacturing company So miyazaki grew up surrounded by airplane and airplane parts.this sparked his love for airplane and aviation,
    * as well as his hatred for engineering.
  • 1947: young miyazaki start grade school
  • 1958:legend of the white serpent is released;inspires miyazaki to follow hobby of animation.
  • 1962:miyazaki graduates from college with degree in economics and political science

Early Career:1963-1965

  • 1963:begin working at toei animation as someone who drew the frame in the between major frame.
  • 1965:Miyazaki becomes the chief animator for prince of the sun,directed by co animator and future studio ghibli partner,Isao Takahata.
  • 1965:Miyazaki marries,fellow animator akemi ota

Moving Among Studio:1965-1980

  • 1971:Miyazaki and Takahata leaves toei animation for a A-pro studios.
  • 1973:Panda!Go,Pand! is released.first film by miyazaki to be created for childrens
  • 1974:Miyazaki and Takahata move to zuiyo pictures.
  • 1974: Alpine Girl Heidi is released; first film where Miyazaki had creative autonomy,
  • 1978: Miyazaki directs most successful television show, Future Boy Conan

It Begins:1982-1985

  • 1982: Miyazaki begins release of his most successful manga, Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind
  • 1984: Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind is released as an anime
  • 1985: Miyazaki and Takahata open their own animation studio in Tokyo, Studio Ghibli

Emergence of studio Ghibli:1985-1999

  • 1986: First Studio Ghibli film, Castle in the Sky, is released
  • 1989:Kiki's Delivery Service released; marks beginning of Miyazaki's move overseas
  • wins Animage Anime Grand Prix prize
  • 1996: Miyazaki receives Winsor McCay lifetime achievement award from Hollywood's International Animated Film Society
  • 1997: Princess Mononoke is released; becomes most successful movie of its time-surpassed record for highest grossing movie in Japan

New Milennium:2000-present

  • 2000: Miyazaki's most successful film, Spirited Away is released
  • 2004: Howl's Moving Castle released
  • 2005: Miyazaki received the Golden Lion lifetime achievement award at the Venice International Film Festival in 2005
  • 2008: Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea is released
  • 2013: Miyazaki's rumored last film, The Wind Rises, is released

Achievements
* Spirited Away (2000)

  • Held record for highest grossing animated film for 15 years
  • Won Best Picture at the Japan Academy Awards
  • Took four Annie's at the 20th Annie Awards for best picture, direction, writing, and music
  • Named best animated feature of 2002 by New York Film Critics Circle, Los Angeles Film Critics Association, Online Film Critics Society, and National Board of Review of Motion Picture
  • Won Oscar for Best Animated Feature at the 75th Academy Awards

Howl's Moving Castle (2004)

  • Became Miyazaki's second highest-grossing film
  • Won Golden Osella Award for animation technology in Venice International Film Festival
  • Nominated for best feature at the Oscars

ponyo(2008)

  • Became his third highest grossing film (gained over $3.5 million opening week)
  • Won four awards at the 8th Annual Tokyo Anime Awards, including "Anime of the Year", "Best Domestic Feature", "Best Director", and "Best Original Story."
  • Won two awards at 32nd Japan Academy Prize: "Animation of the Year" and "Outstanding Achievement in Music"
